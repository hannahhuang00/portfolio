Overview

Given a budget of $100, I worked in a team of four students to help patients with hemiplegia independently put on their shoes over an ankle foot orthosis (AFO). Hemiplegia is paralysis or weakness on half of a person's body (left or right), and hemiplegic patients often wear AFOs to brace to their ankles and help them walk. Our client was an occupational therapist from the Shirley Ryan Ability Lab in Chicago, and she told us that putting on shoes was one of the greatest barriers for hemiplegic patients to gain full independence. We identified needs for the solution such as must be useable with one hand, durable, and versatile for different shoes. This project was completed as part of a 10-week class during freshman year (2019).

Final Design & Deliverables

For the final prototype, we used a black leather flap with weather resistant velcro. Leather was chosen based on interviews with a fabric store and two shoe repair shops, which all cited leather's durability and waterproofness. Additionally, leather gives a minimal, inconspicuous design that is important to not draw attention to the shoe modification. To make this shoe modification accessible and adjustable, we instead made an instruction manual on how to get any shoe of your choosing modified by a shoe repair shop. The schematic I drew is shown. We also gave the final prototype to the user we had worked with the most.
