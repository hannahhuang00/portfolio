HANNAH HUANG'S PORTFOLIO

Force Test Assembly

Human Design • SolidWorks

Overview

During the Summer of 2021, I designed and drew a SolidWorks assembly to measure the max tensile load capacity of a screw with a threaded cap (Sammys®) as an ITW intern. This assembly would be used on the plant floor by machine operators at least once an hour during the production of the parts. Because the users were everyday operators and not quality analysis testers, using this assembly had to be easy, quick, and—most importantly—safe. It features:

  • A diamond grip jaw that clamps onto the thread of the screw.
  • A hydraulic that pump pulls the jaws upwards until the screw fails.
  • A load cell records the maximum force applied.
  • An acrylic panel on the top half of the cage door to ensure safety of the operator from possible projectiles.
  • A light-based feedback system that would indicate when the hydraulic was fully down to reduce the risk of the pinch point beneath the claw. This is still not fool-proofed, and I suggested that they improve the mechanism to prevent opening the door until the hydraulic is fully down.
I worked with a third-party machinist to assemble the final design. I was fortunate enough to visit the factory again in 2023, right after I graduated college. It was great to see the tester still being used.
