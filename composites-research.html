Overview

I started this project during the summer of 2020, coming fresh out of freshman year, at the start of COVID. Professor Wing Kam Liu was an early pioneer in "Mechanicstic Data Science," in which mechanistic models (known equations and mechanical behavior) are combined with empirical models (purely statistical and repeated observation). This combination allows for complex predictions without the need for nearly as much data that purely empirical models require. With little knowlege at the time, I was lucky that Professor Liu taught me general data science and neural networks from scratch and entrusted me to my own research project with guidance from one of his PhD students. I applied mechanistic data science to inform the creation of composite materials, and published in Summer of 2022, at the end of my junior year.

    What I did:
  • Generated 15,000 stress-strain responses for 12 different unidirectional fiber-matrix composite materials.
  • Extracted 17 features from stress-strain results.
  • Reduced dimension of dataset from 15,000 to 3,000 datapoints by averaging 5 microstructure realizations.
  • Reduced dimension of features from 17 to 4 through principal component analysis (PCA).
  • Created, trained, and validated a Feed Forward Neural Network (FFNN).
  • Predicted composite compositions for desired material properties to inform composite creation.
  • Published article with first-author designation in Computational Materials Science, "Knowledge database creation for design of polymer matrix composite"

Tools & Software

MATLAB

  • Used MATLAB-based FEA package developed by lab to simulate stress-strain responses.

Python
