Jack-Box Simulation

Overview

In this project, I performed a 2D simualtion of the movement of a 4-pointed "jack" in a box, where the box has an upwards external force (ensuring that the box does not constantly accelerate downwards due to gravity or collision). This model assumes completely elastic collisions. The box is set to be 10 times heavier than the jack, such that the jack experiences more movement due to impact. I completed this project in Sophomore year (2021).

    What I did:
  • Calculated frame transformations, to be used in all equations.
  • Derived Lagrangian and Euler-Lagrange eqautions from kinetic & potential energy.
  • Derived impact equation to be used when impact is detected.
  • Numerically solved equations of motion with Runge-Kutta 4th-order method (RK4).
  • Verified conservation of momentum by ensuring the change in Hamiltonian is zero at each impact.
  • Used Python to solve complex matrix equations and simulate/animate system.
